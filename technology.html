How we build

Our Technology

Precision manufacturing using filament winding, advanced composite curing and in-house burst testing — all meeting the highest ISO standards.

Process 01

Filament Winding

Filament winding is a precision technique for crafting Type IV pressure vessels — wrapping carbon fibre strands around a cylindrical mandrel to create strong yet lightweight structures. Ideal for high-strength applications, this method ensures durability and efficiency.

Materials

Type IV pressure vessels utilise filament winding combining carbon fibres with epoxy resin for enhanced strength and durability. This advanced construction ensures high mechanical properties, including tensile strength and corrosion resistance — resulting in lightweight vessels with superior gas containment capabilities.

The Process

Filament winding delivers precise fibre placement and uniform distribution, creating strong, lightweight Type IV pressure vessels ideal for storing and transporting Hydrogen and CNG gases with exceptional mechanical properties.

Process 02

Composite Curing Oven

Our composite curing oven is essential for crafting high-pressure vessels for Hydrogen and CNG (Type IV). Following plastic liner moulding, the process progresses to filament winding — where fibres are precisely applied to the liner using an automated procedure that ensures uniform fibre placement.

The curing oven is then used to fully cure the composite vessel under precisely controlled conditions.

1

Preheating

Remove moisture from the vessel before curing begins.

2

Loading

The vessel is loaded into the oven under controlled conditions.

3

Heating to Cure Temperature

Precise temperature control ensures optimal resin curing.

4

Soaking

Maintaining the temperature for optimal composite performance.

5

Controlled Cooling

Gradual cooling minimises internal stresses in the material.

6

Quality Inspection & Unloading

Final quality check before the vessel moves to the next stage.

Process 03

Burst Test Facility

The burst test facility is crucial for ensuring the quality of Type IV pressure vessels for Hydrogen and CNG. By subjecting vessels to extreme conditions, it verifies compliance with safety standards — reducing risks in storage and transportation.

In addition to burst testing, we are able to perform further custom testing procedures depending on specific project or certification requirements. Contact us to discuss your needs.

1

Test Chamber

A reinforced chamber with full safety measures isolates the test vessel.

2

Pressure Control

Precise pressure control system ramps up incrementally.

3

Data Monitoring

Instruments continuously record pressure, temperature and deformation.

4

Burst Test

Pressure increases until failure — revealing the true safety margin.

5

Data Analysis & Compliance

Results are compared against ISO and industry standards for certification.
